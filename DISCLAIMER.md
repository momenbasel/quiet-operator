# Legal Disclaimer & Acceptable Use

This repository documents **adversary tradecraft for authorized red team operations,
purple team exercises, and blue team detection engineering.** It exists so that
operators and defenders speak the same language about how stealthy activity looks
on the wire and on the host.

## Authorized use only

You may use this material **only** when **all** of the following are true:

1. You have **written authorization** (a signed Rules of Engagement / Statement of
   Work / scope letter) from the legal owner of every system in scope.
2. Your activity stays **inside the agreed scope, time window, and data-handling
   rules** of that authorization.
3. You are operating as part of a sanctioned security engagement, internal red/purple
   team, CTF, lab, or personal test environment that **you own**.

## Prohibited use

Do **not** use this material to access, persist on, move through, or exfiltrate data
from any system you are not explicitly authorized to test. Unauthorized access,
interception of communications, and data theft are crimes in most jurisdictions
(e.g. US CFAA 18 U.S.C. § 1030, UK Computer Misuse Act 1990, EU national equivalents).
The techniques here are dual-use; intent and authorization are what separate a red
team operator from a criminal.

## Defensive intent

Every offensive technique in this repository is paired with a **Detection & telemetry**
section describing the artifacts it generates and how a defender catches it. If you
are a defender, read the detection sections first — they are written for you.
